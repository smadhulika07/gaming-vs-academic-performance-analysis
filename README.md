# Gaming vs Academic Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores the relationship between gaming habits and academic performance among students using SQL-based Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA). The analysis investigates how factors such as gaming hours, addiction levels, sleep duration, attendance, study hours, stress levels, and social activity may influence academic outcomes.

## Objectives

* Analyze student gaming behavior and its potential impact on academic performance.
* Identify patterns between gaming addiction and study habits.
* Examine the relationship between sleep, stress, and grades.
* Explore the effects of attendance and social activities on academic outcomes.
* Generate data-driven insights and recommendations for students and educational institutions.

## Dataset Features

The dataset includes information related to:

* Age
* Gender
* Gaming Hours
* Gaming Genre
* Addiction Score
* Study Hours
* Sleep Hours

* Stress Level

## Key Findings

* Students with higher addiction scores generally spend more time gaming.
* Increased gaming hours may be associated with lower academic performance.
* Students who study more tend to achieve better grades.
* Adequate sleep is associated with improved academic outcomes and lower stress levels.
* Higher attendance percentages generally correlate with better grades.
* Certain gaming genres show higher addiction tendencies among students.
* Stress levels vary across different age groups.

## Recommendations and Insights

### 1. Balanced Gaming Habits

Students with higher addiction scores tend to spend more time gaming, which may negatively affect academic performance. Educational institutions should encourage balanced gaming schedules and promote healthy digital habits among students.

### 2. Importance of Healthy Sleep

Reduced sleep hours may be associated with higher stress levels and lower grades. Students should be encouraged to maintain proper sleep routines to improve both mental health and academic outcomes.

### 3. Stress Management Support

Certain age groups experience higher average stress levels. Colleges and universities can introduce counseling services, stress-management workshops, and wellness programs to support student mental health.

### 4. Improving Student Attendance

Students with higher attendance percentages generally achieve better academic performance. Institutions can implement attendance awareness programs and engagement activities to improve participation in classes.

### 5. Encouraging Healthy Social Activities

Social activity appears to influence academic performance and stress levels. Students should be encouraged to participate in balanced extracurricular and social activities that support overall well-being.

### 6. Gaming Addiction Awareness

Some gaming genres appear to have higher addiction tendencies among students. Awareness programs on responsible gaming and digital wellness can help students develop healthier gaming behaviors.

### 7. Better Time Management

Students spending excessive time on gaming may dedicate less time to studying. Time-management training and productivity guidance can help students maintain a better balance between academics and entertainment.

### 8. Data-Driven Student Monitoring

Educational institutions can use analytics dashboards and student behavioral data to identify at-risk students early and provide timely academic or psychological support.
